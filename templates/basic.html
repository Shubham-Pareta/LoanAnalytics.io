Key Features Analyzed

  • Number of Dependents: The number of individuals financially dependent on the applicant. More dependents might indicate a higher financial burden.
  • Annual Income: The total income earned by the applicant in a year. Higher income usually increases the likelihood of loan approval.
  • Loan Amount: The amount of money requested by the applicant. Larger loan amounts require careful assessment against the applicant’s financial stability.
  • Loan Term: The duration for which the loan is taken. Longer terms can mean higher interest rates and affect the approval decision.
  • CIBIL Score: A measure of the applicant’s creditworthiness. Higher scores reflect better credit history and increase the chances of approval.
  • Asset Values: The total value of the applicant’s assets, including residential, commercial, luxury, and bank assets. Higher asset values provide more security for the loan.
  • Education Level: Indicates whether the applicant is a graduate or not. Education level can influence income potential and financial stability.
  • Self Employment Status: Whether the applicant is self-employed. Self-employment status can impact income stability and loan approval.
